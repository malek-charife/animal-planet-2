Fun facts

Sharks have been around for over 400 million years, making them older than dinosaurs. Some species can live for 25-40 years, with the Greenland shark possibly reaching over 400 years. They can detect electrical fields through special organs called ampullae of Lorenzini. Despite their reputation, sharks pose minimal threat to humans, with around 70-100 shark attacks annually worldwide. Some sharks, like the great white, breach out of water while hunting. Their skeletons are made of cartilage, not bone, aiding in agility and speed.

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, with necks up to 6 feet long, yet they have the same number of neck vertebrae as humans. They only need 5 to 30 minutes of sleep a day, often standing up. Their tongues can extend over 18 inches and are bluish-black to prevent sunburn. Giraffes' hearts weigh around 25 pounds and pump blood up their long necks, thanks to powerful valves preventing blood from rushing to their heads. They communicate through infrasound, low-frequency noises below human hearing range.

Lions are the only truly social cats, living in prides of up to 30 individuals. They can roar loudly enough to be heard up to 5 miles away. Females are the primary hunters, relying on teamwork to bring down prey like zebras and buffalo. Cubs start learning to hunt at around 11 months old. Male lions defend their territory and offspring from intruders. They have powerful retractable claws and can reach speeds of up to 50 mph in short bursts. Lions spend about 16-20 hours a day resting or sleeping.
